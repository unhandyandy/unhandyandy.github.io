Messages.


The goal is to have the most pieces on the board when the game ends. Pieces move like chess rooks - vertically or horizontally any distance without jumping over any other pieces. Capturing is a variation on the custodial version: after player A moves a piece, any piece of player B which borders it (vertically or horizontally) and which has 2 or more of player A's pieces as neighbors is removed from the board. The other move possible is birth: pieces can be born on the white square at the each player's near right. The birthing square are particular to each player, e.g. player A cannot birth his pieces on player B's birthing square. Passing is allowed, and the game ends with consecutive passes.
